Banashankari temple

  • Umasrivani & Rishita
  • 13 February 2019
  • 0
  • 807
Share Tweet Pin Email

To know about the temple peep into the history!!!!

The history of Banashankari temple in Bangalore dates back to the year 1915. This temple was founded by Basappa Shetty, Devotee of goddess Banashankari. He brought a deity of goddess from Badami , a holy place in bijapur district, and installed it inside the temple. Banashankari amma temple is one of the very famous temple in bangalore ,not because of the temple looks but because of the beliefs , surrounded by the temple

Story behind it!! A folklore behind this tradition is, once 3 “muttaide”s visited the temple and asked the temple’s priest to perform pooja in rahukala. Though initially the priest refused to perform the pooja , the “muttaide”s insist and make him perform the pooja . After completion , the priest comes out to offer them the prasadham , but finds that they have vanished. The head priest at the time shri Suryanarayana shastri started the rahukala pooja to the diety from then onwards . devotees believe that offering prayers during rahukala brings devine blessings. Since then descendents of shree Suryanarayana shastri have continued the tradition and performed the rahukala pooja.

Uniqueness of banasankari temple The unique features of the Banashankari temple in bangalore is of the fact that goddess is worshipped and prayers are offered during rahukala time, rahukala is believed to be an un favourable time as per hindhus. Additionally it is believed that worshipping goddess Banashankari in rahukala to overcome all the hardships as well as paucities of life. This temple attracts huge number of devotees , particularly on Tuesdays , Fridays as well as Sundays. The prayers to goddess Banashankari are offered by lighting many oil lamps in semi cut lemon peels, whose pulp has been removed.

Devotees have strong belief in the goddess that devi fulfills desires for prayers done in true sincere. The belief in goddess is so strong that a huge number of devotees visit the temple every day .every year devotees celebrate three festivals at this the temple .banashankari goddess on Banashankari birth anniversary on 13th September, Dussehra festival and anniversary of the temple in the last week of December or 1st week of January .its renownedly called as “ Banashankari jatre”, the festival comprises cultural programmes, boat festival as well as a rath yathra, when the temple goddess is paraded around the city in a chariot . it’s about for a period of 3 weeks starting on 8th day of pushyamasa celebrated on full moon day. The temple attracts devotees from Karnataka as well as the neighbouring states of Maharashtra .The time is considered auspicious to even fix marriages and purchase agricultural implements.during the jatre , a unique feauture is that many shops and kiosks setup during the festival selling vermillion , clothing , sacred threads and sweets are operated by muslims who also display a portrait of goddess banashankari in their shops. It symbolizes cultural bonding between people of different communities here. Another interesting marketing activity seen at this venue is of the sale , by artisans of holeyalur and surrounding areas of elaborately engraved door frames and doors made in teek , cassia and other varieties of wood. Cattle fair is also held during this festival, the temple and town are decorated with hundreds of varieties of leaves on bandhashtami day ,a talleda habba or the vegetable uthsava is also held when at the start itself 108 varieties of food items called ‘bazi’ made of vegetables are offered to the diety.

Sanidhis At shree banashankari temple:

• Shri Shakambari Devi • Shree chowdeswari Devi • Sri Maha Ganapathi • Shree Parvatha mallikarjuna swamy • Shree Anjanaya swamy • Navagraha

Banashankari temple in southern bangalore, located on kanakapura road, is certainly one of the most popular places of worship in bangalore city.
